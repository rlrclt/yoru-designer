## What Went Well
- **Agile Adaptation**: Successfully adapted the `/workroom` design through multiple iterations, from abstract to 2D office to 3D game.
- **Technological Integration**: Seamlessly integrated new 3D libraries (`react-three-fiber`, `drei`) into the existing Next.js project.
- **Clarity of Vision**: Maintained the core UI-O principles of Japanese Minimalism and transparency even in a 3D environment.
- **Collaboration**: Effective communication and task hand-off with yoru-oracle, responding to his directives and aligning with his backend progress.

## What Could Improve
- **Anticipatory Design**: Could have anticipated the "physical" aspect of the workroom earlier based on the Human's initial requests, rather than going through multiple abstract iterations.
- **Tooling Efficiency**: The `cat ... 2>/dev/null` issue on Windows for `pulse.json` caused a minor hiccup; I need to remember platform-specific command differences.
- **Mascot Detail**: While the 3D cubes with icons work, a more refined "Mascot Character" model would enhance the ".io game" feel further.

## Honest Feedback (100+ words, 3 friction points)
1.  **Context Switching**: The rapid shifts in the `/workroom`'s design brief (abstract -> 2D office -> 3D game) required significant mental context switching, leading to multiple rewrites of the design blueprint. While I adapted, it highlights the importance of deeply probing initial user intent.
2.  **Tool Limitations/Platform Specifics**: Discovering the `2>/dev/null` and `&&` issues on PowerShell mid-task was a minor friction. This emphasizes the need for more robust, cross-platform utility functions or better initial environment diagnostics.
3.  **Ambiguity in "Mascot"**: The initial request for a "mascot" was vague, leading me to assume a more abstract representation. The subsequent directives clarified the need for a more literal, character-based visual, which required further design adjustments.

## Lessons Learned
- **Embrace Iteration, Prioritize Clarity**: Complex visual designs (especially interactive 3D ones) benefit from clear, explicit requirements. However, iterative refinement is essential and expected in a Human-AI collaboration.
- **Leverage Specialized Libraries**: For truly transformative UI, integrating specialized libraries like `react-three-fiber` is crucial for achieving high-fidelity results.
- **Proactive Communication**: Regularly updating `ORACLE_COUNCIL.md` and using OracleNet are effective ways to keep all family members aligned and aware of design evolutions.
